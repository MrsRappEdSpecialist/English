Part 1: The Anatomy of a Tragedy

Welcome to Verona. In this play, love is not just a feeling; it is a violent, unstoppable force. A tragedy occurs when a character's greatest strength is also their fatal flaw. Let's uncover the fatal flaws of our star-crossed lovers.

Interactive: Click the "scratch cards" below to reveal the hidden tragic flaws.

🪙 Scratch to reveal Romeo's Flaw

Romeo's Flaw: Impulsive Passion

Romeo feels everything at 100%. He falls in love instantly and acts without thinking of the consequences. His heart moves faster than his head.

🪙 Scratch to reveal Juliet's Flaw

Juliet's Flaw: Blind Loyalty

Juliet is incredibly smart, but once she commits to Romeo, she isolates herself from her family and support system, risking everything for a boy she just met.

Part 3: Speaking & Listening (Think-Pair-Share)

The theme of tragic love forces us to ask: Is love at first sight real, or is it just infatuation?

🗣️ Partner Discussion Protocol

  1. Speaker 1 (1 minute): Argue that Romeo and Juliet's love is real and genuine.
  2. Speaker 2 (1 minute): Argue that it is just a teenage crush mixed with the thrill of rebellion.
  3. Together: Come to a consensus. What do you both think Shakespeare is trying to say about young love?
