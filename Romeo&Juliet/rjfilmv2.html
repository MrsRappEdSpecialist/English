Two households, both alike in dignity,

Module 3: The Party (Act 1, Scene 5)

📌 CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.9-10.3: Analyze how complex characters develop over the course of a text.
📌 Focus: Tone Shift, Dramatic Irony
👆 Click to Reveal Modern Translation
ROMEO: If I profane with my unworthiest hand
This holy shrine, the gentle fine is this:
My lips, two blushing pilgrims, ready stand
To smooth that rough touch with a tender kiss.

JULIET: My only love sprung from my only hate!
Too early seen unknown, and known too late!
MODERN:
ROMEO: If my hand is unworthy when it touches your hand (this holy place), then my lips are two blushing pilgrims, ready to make things better with a kiss.

JULIET: The only man I love is the son of the only man I hate! I saw him too early without knowing who he was, and I found out who he was too late!

Activity 1: Diction & Metaphor

Romeo compares his lips to "pilgrims" and Juliet's hand to a "holy shrine." What tone does this create?

Module 6: The Ultimatum (Act 3, Scene 5)

📌 CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.9-10.4: Analyze tone.
📌 Focus: Power Dynamics, Toxic Traits
👆 Click to Reveal Modern Translation
CAPULET: Hang thee, young baggage! disobedient wretch!
I tell thee what: get thee to church o' Thursday,
Or never after look me in the face.
...And you be mine, I'll give you to my friend;
And you be not, hang, beg, starve, die in the streets.
MODERN:
CAPULET: Forget about you, you worthless girl! You disobedient wretch! I’ll tell you what: go to the church on Thursday or never look me in the face again... If you are my daughter, I will give you to my friend Paris. If you don't agree, you can hang, beg, starve, and die in the streets.

Activity 1: Tone Analysis

Module 8: Tragedy & Peace (Act 5, Scene 3)

📌 CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.9-10.2: Determine a theme.
📌 Focus: Resolution, Consequences
👆 Click to Reveal Modern Translation
CAPULET: O brother Montague, give me thy hand:
This is my daughter's jointure.
MONTAGUE: For I will raise her statue in pure gold.
PRINCE: A glooming peace this morning with it brings;
The sun, for sorrow, will not show his head:
...For never was a story of more woe
Than this of Juliet and her Romeo.
MODERN:
CAPULET: Oh, brother Montague, give me your hand. This is my daughter’s dowry.
MONTAGUE: I will raise a statue of her in pure gold.
PRINCE: This morning brings a gloomy peace. The sun is too sad to show its face... For there was never a story more full of pain than the story of Juliet and her Romeo.
