Before We Begin: The World of the Play

Scaffold: Setting the stage

Imagine a beautiful Italian city called Verona — think narrow stone streets, grand houses, hot summers, and two powerful families who absolutely hate each other.

This isn't just a love story. It's a story about what happens when the people around you won't stop fighting — and you're caught in the middle.

📌 Key Context

  • • Written by William ShakespeareEnglish playwright, 1564–1616. Wrote 37+ plays! around 1597
  • • Set in Verona, Italy — a real city, but the story is fiction
  • • The play is a tragedyA type of story that ends in suffering or death for the main characters — meaning it does NOT end happily
  • • Two wealthy families — the Montagues and the Capulets — are in a bitter, long-running feud

💬 Think-Pair-Share: Quick Discussion

Before we meet the characters, discuss with a partner:

"Have you ever been in a situation where two groups of people didn't get along, and you were stuck in the middle? What was that like?"

⚔️ Two Households, Both Alike in Dignity...

That's the very first line of the play. It means both families are equally powerful and important — which is exactly why their feud is so destructive.

🔵

House Montague

Romeo's Family
  • • One of the two most powerful families in Verona
  • • Romeo is their only son — their whole future
  • • They worry about Romeo's moodiness
  • • Includes Romeo's friends and loyal servants
🔴

House Capulet

Juliet's Family
  • • Equally powerful — fierce rivals of the Montagues
  • • Juliet is their only daughter — just 13 years old
  • • Her father is planning her marriage already
  • • Very protective of family honor and reputation

🤔 Scaffold: Modern Parallel

Think of this like two rival families in a TV drama or movie — maybe The Outsiders (Socs vs. Greasers), rival neighborhoods, or even sports rivals taken way too far. The key: nobody remembers why the feud started, but everyone keeps fighting anyway.

💬 Group Discussion Prompt

"Why do you think people keep fighting even when no one remembers how it started? What would it take to end a feud like this?"
